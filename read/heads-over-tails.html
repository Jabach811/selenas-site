Heads Over Tails · Book Two

Chapter One

The ring was still in her coat pocket, and that was the whole problem in a nutshell. Two days now it had been riding around with her — to work, to the store, to church and back — and she still hadn't decided whether it meant the start of something or the end of it.

You would think a question that big would announce itself. You would think it would come with trumpets, or at least a clear yes glowing somewhere behind your ribs. Instead it just sat there, small and gold and heavier than it had any right to be.

"You've stirred that coffee about forty times," her sister said, sliding into the booth across from her. "Either you're trying to wear a hole in the cup or you've got something to tell me. Knowing you, it's both."

She opened her mouth, closed it, and laughed instead — that helpless laugh that shows up right when you're trying hardest to be serious. Some news you can't say sitting down. Some you can't say at all until somebody makes you.

"Okay," she said finally. "Okay. But you have to promise not to make that face." Her sister immediately made the face. And that, more than anything, was how she knew she'd come to the right table to fall apart at.
